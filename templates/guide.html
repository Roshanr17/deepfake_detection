User Guide

Getting Started

Follow these simple steps to detect deepfakes in your videos:

1

Upload Your Video

Click on the upload area or drag and drop your video file. We support MP4, MOV, AVI, WebM, and MKV formats.

2

Wait for Analysis

Our AI system will analyze the video frames. Processing time depends on video length, usually 30 seconds to 2 minutes.

3

Review Results

Get detailed analysis including confidence scores, probability distribution, and explanations for the prediction.

Understanding Results

Confidence Score

The confidence score ranges from 0% to 100% and indicates how confident our system is in its prediction.

90-100% Confidence High confidence. The prediction is very reliable. Use this result with confidence.
70-90% Confidence Good confidence. The prediction is likely reliable. Recommended for most applications.
50-70% Confidence Moderate confidence. Results are less certain. Consider additional verification.
Below 50% Confidence Low confidence. Result is uncertain. Definitely verify through other means.

Best Practices

Good Practice: Use videos with clear facial visibility for best results.
Good Practice: For multiple faces, the system analyzes the most prominent face.
Warning: Very short videos (<2 seconds) may have less reliable results.
Warning: Heavily compressed or low-resolution videos may affect accuracy.
Important: Videos with no detected faces will be rejected. Ensure your video contains at least one visible face.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How accurate is your detector? A: Our system achieves 95%+ accuracy on standard deepfake datasets. However, accuracy can vary based on video quality and deepfake technique used.
Q: What happens to my uploaded videos? A: Videos are processed temporarily and deleted automatically after analysis. We don't store or use your videos for any other purpose.
Q: How long does analysis take? A: Typical analysis takes 30 seconds to 2 minutes depending on video length and server load. Very long videos may take longer.
Q: Can I use this for legal purposes? A: This tool is for informational purposes. For legal proceedings, consult with forensic experts and use multiple verification methods.
Q: What video length is recommended? A: Videos between 30 seconds and 10 minutes work best. Very short (<30 seconds) or very long (>30 minutes) videos may have varying results.

Technical Details

Model Architecture

We use MobileNet-v3 as the frame feature extractor, which provides:

  • Efficient deep learning model
  • Fast inference on CPU
  • High accuracy with lower computational cost

Processing Pipeline

1. Video Input → Frame Extraction
2. Face Detection via Cascade Classifiers
3. Face Preprocessing & Normalization
4. Frame Analysis via MobileNet-v3
5. Classification & Confidence Scoring
6. Result Aggregation & Output
